American Craft Beer Styles

Project Introduction

Beer can be divided into many styles, each style has a different history and characteristics. There are many different beer styles to choose from on the American craft beer market. So, what is the difference between different styles of beer? Which states are rich in craft beerï¼ŸLet us take a look at these different styles of beer from different angles.

Data Source

This project is based off a data set found on Kaggle.com. This data set contains a list of 2,410 American craft beers and 510 American breweries. It was collected from CraftCans.com in January 2017. The data contains many variables. This visualization only uses variables related to the project theme, including: style, ABV, IBU, and state.

Process

The process starts with data analysis performed in R. The interactive visualization was created using D3.JS. Non-interactive visualizations are created in R using ggplot2 and exported to vector design software for visual optimization.

Table of Content

Design Rationale

The tree map shows the different beer styles in the market. The area of each rectangle represents the number of products of the style. The percentage of each style (the number of products of that style divided by the total number of products) were added to help readers perceive the quantitative differences between styles. The tree map was selected because it shows both the variety and the proportion. This align with my purpose, which is to show the diversity of beer styles and then identify some of the most common styles for later analysis.

Data Analysis

All the entries of beer products was grouped by the styles. In order to make the Tree map more readable, some beer styles with infrequent entires were filtered out from this visualizations. The threshold is set to be 30 products. If a styles has less than 30 products on the market, then it is considered less relevant. Five beer styles with the most beer products were screened out during this process and used as a focused group in the following visualizations.

What are the differences in the bitterness of different styles of beer?

International Bitterness Unit (IBU)

IBU is a standard for measuring the bitterness of beer. The IBU rating can be used as a general guide to taste, with a lower IBU indicating less bitterness, and vice versa. However, we must bear in mind that IBU is the only factor that determines the bitterness of beer taste. Malt and other flavors can mask the bitter taste in beer.

Strength of beer: Are there differences in the alcohol content of different styles of beer?

Alcohol by volume (ABV)

Alcohol by volume (ABV) is a standard measure of how much alcohol (ethanol) is contained in a given volume of an alcoholic beverage, it is expressed as a volume percent. It is defined as the number of millilitres of pure ethanol present in 100 ml.

Design Rationale

Similar to the line chart above, the five most common beer styles are compared. The x-axis represents the alcohol content, and the y-axis represents the number of products. On the x-axis, the scale used is alcohol by volume (ABV); the greater the number, the stronger the beer. In daily life, the most common form of ABV is percentage, where 2% alcohol content is equal to 0.02 ABV. From this chart, you can see the product quantity distribution of each beer in different alcohol strength ranges.

Design Rationale

This visualization uses a scatter plot to show the alcohol content and bitterness of the 5 styles of beer. The x-axis represents bitterness (IBU) and the y-axis represents alcohol content (ABV). The color coding here is used to distinguish different beer styles.The audience can also select the "beer style" to be displayed by clicking the filter.

On the scatter plot, we can compare bitterness and alcohol content at the same time to see the relationship between the two variables. It can be seen that bitterness is positively correlated with alcohol content, and beer with higher alcohol content is usually more bitter.

Where are the five most common beer styles produced in the United States?

Explanation

Above is a grouped bar chart with a circular layout. The data is divided into five groups based on five beer styles, showing the number of beer products produced in different states. From the figure, we can see that Colorado and California produce more beer. Especially Colorado, it is the largest producer of the four styles.

Design Rationale

Colors are used to encode different styles. In group, the bars are arranged in descending order. In this way, we can see which states produce more. A advantage of circular layout is efficient use of space. One disadvantage of the circular layout is that the bar data is more difficult to read. It is necessary to use more prominent auxiliary lines, thus, the circular grids are drawn over the bars.
